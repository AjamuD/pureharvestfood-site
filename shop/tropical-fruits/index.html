Our Dehydration Process

Learn how we transform fresh tropical fruits into delicious, nutritious dehydrated snacks while preserving their natural flavor and nutrients.

From Farm to Package

1

Careful Selection

We partner with local farmers to source the freshest, ripest fruits at their peak flavor. Each fruit is hand-selected to ensure quality.

2

Washing & Preparation

Fruits are thoroughly washed and carefully sliced to ensure even dehydration. No preservatives or additives are used in our process.

3

Slow Dehydration

Our fruits are slowly dehydrated at low temperatures to preserve nutrients, enzymes, and flavor while removing moisture.

4

Quality Control

Each batch undergoes rigorous quality testing for moisture content, flavor, and appearance before being carefully packaged.

Our Heritage

Discover the rich history of tropical fruit preservation in Trinidad and Tobago, and how Pure Harvest continues this tradition with modern techniques.

A Legacy of Preservation

For generations, the people of Trinidad and Tobago have practiced the art of fruit preservation, developing techniques to extend the life of seasonal tropical fruits. This tradition was born from necessity in times before modern refrigeration, allowing communities to enjoy their beloved fruits year-round.

In 2022, Pure Harvest was established with a vision to revive and modernize these traditional preservation methods. We combine time-honored techniques with modern technology to create premium dehydrated fruits that maintain their nutritional value and authentic taste.

Traditional Roots

Tropical Smoothie Recipes

Create delicious and nutritious smoothies using our dehydrated fruits. These recipes are perfect for a quick breakfast or post-workout refreshment.

Tropical Paradise Smoothie

A refreshing blend of mango and papaya with coconut water.

Ingredients:

  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated mango
  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated papaya
  • • 1 cup coconut water
  • • 1 banana
  • • 1 tbsp honey (optional)
  • • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Soak dehydrated fruits in coconut water for 10 minutes
  2. Add all ingredients to blender
  3. Blend until smooth
  4. Serve immediately

Energy Boost Smoothie

A protein-rich blend with banana and star fruit.

Ingredients:

  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated banana
  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated star fruit
  • • 1 cup almond milk
  • • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Soak dehydrated fruits in warm water for 10 minutes
  2. Combine all ingredients in blender
  3. Blend until creamy
  4. Top with additional chia seeds

Caribbean Green Smoothie

A nutritious blend featuring sapodilla and golden apple.

Ingredients:

  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated sapodilla
  • • 2 tbsp dehydrated golden apple
  • • 2 cups spinach
  • • 1 cup coconut water
  • • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Soak dehydrated fruits in coconut water
  2. Blend spinach and liquid first
  3. Add remaining ingredients and blend
  4. Garnish with mint leaves
